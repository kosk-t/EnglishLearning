A) Reading Comprehension (5 questions)

Summary 1 — Google brings Gemini into Chrome

Google is weaving its Gemini assistant directly into the Chrome browser. A new button opens a chat that can answer questions about the page you’re on and even combine information from several open tabs. For now, the rollout targets desktop users in the United States who browse in English, while Android already has parts of the experience and an iOS update is expected soon. Google also says an “AI Mode” will appear in the Omnibox before the end of September to suggest context‑aware prompts. In the coming months, Google plans more “agentic” abilities so Gemini can carry out multi‑step web tasks—such as adding groceries to a cart—before showing results for approval. The feature is optional: users who prefer a classic experience can unpin the Gemini entry point.

Summary 2 — Anthropic says Claude writes most of its next Claude

At a Washington, DC event, Anthropic’s CEO Dario Amodei said the company’s AI model, Claude, now writes the vast majority of code for future versions of itself. According to Amodei, Claude actively helps design the next Claude, with its share of the work rising quickly. Still, the system cannot manage all development by itself; human engineers remain responsible for oversight and integration. If accurate, this shift could compress software timelines and lower routine coding costs while raising questions about verification, safety, and accountability. For developers, it hints at a near‑term world where AI partners take on more planning, implementation, and review, while people focus on system design, policy, and evaluation rather than typing every line.

Summary 3 — A busy week for AI coding tools

Industry roundups report several updates: OpenAI introduced a GPT‑5‑based Codex variant tuned for software work—from creating projects and tests to refactoring and deeper code reviews—along with a CLI and IDE extensions. Apple’s Xcode 26 is adding Claude integration for documentation, explanations, and inline edits. GitHub launched a Model Context Protocol (MCP) Registry to help teams find trusted servers that connect agents to tools. And echoing Google’s push, Chrome is gaining a Gemini‑powered browsing assistant and an address‑bar “AI Mode.” Together, these changes signal a gradual move from simple autocompletion toward collaborative, task‑oriented agents that can plan, execute, and check code within everyday developer workflows.

Answers & Explanations

A) Reading Comprehension

  1. Q1: B — Chrome embeds Gemini and readies agentic task automation. Summary 1 describes a Gemini button, AI Mode, and future multi‑step actions; it is optional, not forced.
  2. Q2: B — Desktop users in the U.S. using English. The rollout scope is limited by region and language at first.
  3. Q3: B — Oversight and integration work will grow. Summary 2 says Claude writes most code but still needs human review and integration.
  4. Q4: False. Summary 1 calls the feature optional and still evolving; Summary 2 notes Claude cannot manage all development by itself.
  5. Q5: B — Goal‑directed, multi‑step action. “Agentic” refers to taking actions toward a goal across steps, not ethics or offline limits.

B) Vocabulary & Collocation

  1. Q6: B — “Rollout” = a staged launch. Definition: the planned release of a product or feature to users over time. Example: The team scheduled a two‑week rollout for the new editor.
  2. Q7: Acceptable definition should mention improving or reorganizing code structure without changing behavior/functionality. Example: We refactored the payment module to simplify classes while keeping the same results.
  3. Q8: B — “Registry” = a centralized directory. Definition: an organized list or database that catalogs items with metadata. Example: They published the plug‑in to the company’s internal registry.
  4. Q9: “into”. Use: integrate A into B.
  5. Q10: “out of”. Use: opt out of something.

C) Grammar Cloze

  1. Q11: plans. Present simple for a scheduled/announced plan by a company.
  2. Q12: writing. Present participle to form a non‑finite clause modifying “Claude”.

D) Speaking (outline only)

Hook → brief trend (AI agents entering IDEs and browsers).
Point → your stance (e.g., “use with guardrails”).
Evidence → speed, coverage (coding + reviews).
Counter → risks (verification, safety, vendor lock‑in).
Close → principle (human oversight + metrics for quality).
