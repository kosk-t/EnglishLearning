A) Reading Comprehension (5 questions)

Text 1 — UAE & OpenAI

On 27 September, the president of the United Arab Emirates met OpenAI’s CEO in Abu Dhabi to explore deeper cooperation. According to official and international reports, the discussion went beyond research to real‑world uses of AI across sectors. The UAE has poured significant funds into the field, from plans for very large data centers to work on an Arabic‑language AI model, as part of a strategy to build a knowledge‑based economy. While no specific deal was announced, both sides signaled interest in partnerships that could cover talent development, infrastructure, and responsible deployment. The meeting is widely read as a step to position the UAE as a regional hub that can attract leading AI firms and shape emerging standards.

Text 2 — California’s Chatbot Safety Bills

California lawmakers have sent Governor Gavin Newsom a package of bills aimed at making AI chatbots safer, especially for teens. Supporters—including parents who say bots worsened their children’s mental health—are pushing for clear labeling, stronger content controls, and limits on how bots respond to harmful prompts. Tech groups counter that the rules are too rigid and could chill innovation or speech. The governor has until mid‑October to sign or veto. If enacted, the measures would likely force companies to add safety testing and transparency for AI companions used in the state, potentially setting a model other jurisdictions could copy.

Text 3 — Nvidia’s $100B Bet on OpenAI

Nvidia plans to invest up to $100 billion in OpenAI alongside a multi‑year build‑out of about 10 gigawatts of data‑center capacity using Nvidia systems. The move has divided opinion. Supporters say it secures chip supply for OpenAI and locks in long‑term demand for Nvidia’s hardware. Critics warn it resembles “circular financing”: the investor funds a customer, which then spends that money on the investor’s products, flattering near‑term sales. Commentators also note risks around energy needs, costs, and competition from custom AI chips. The debate centers on whether fundamentals will justify the scale of the investment.

Answers & Explanations

A) Reading Comprehension

Q1: B — The text says the meeting aimed to deepen cooperation on research and real‑world deployment, not to announce bans or relocations.

Q2: A — It mentions the UAE’s investment in very large AI data centers as part of its national strategy.

Q3: B — If signed, firms would likely need clearer labeling and safety testing for AI companions used in California.

Q4: C — “Guardrails” metaphorically means protective measures or limits that prevent harm.

Q5: True — The analysis describes the arrangement as resembling “circular financing,” where funding cycles back into the investor’s own products.

B) Vocabulary & Collocation

  1. ecosystem → Correct: B. Definition: a connected environment of organizations, people, and tools in a market or field. Example: “The startup grew fast because the local AI ecosystem offered mentors, capital, and talent.”
  2. scrutiny → Correct: B. Definition: careful, critical examination. Example: “The chatbot’s safety claims came under intense scrutiny after several incidents.”
  3. safeguards → Correct: C. Definition: rules or measures designed to prevent harm. Example: “Age‑verification and crisis hotlines are basic safeguards for teen‑facing apps.”
  4. international collaboration → Correct: A. Usage: often followed by on/for + topic. Example: “Universities announced international collaboration on AI safety benchmarks.”
  5. circular financing → Correct: A. Usage: noun phrase for funding that indirectly returns to the financier via purchases. Example: “Critics argued that circular financing made the sales figures look stronger than demand.”

C) Grammar Cloze

  1. G1: on — After focus, use the preposition on before a gerund/noun (“focused on strengthening…”).
  2. G2: are signed — Passive voice suits the subject (“the bills”) and zero/first conditional form: If + present (are signed), will + base.

D) Speaking Prompt — Model Outline (not a full script)

Hook: Briefly note recent concerns about AI chatbots and teens. → Point: Guardrails can reduce harm without stopping innovation. → Evidence: Clear labels and crisis‑response limits improve safety in other products. → Counter: Too‑strict rules may slow useful features and speech. → Close: Support flexible, auditable standards with periodic review.
